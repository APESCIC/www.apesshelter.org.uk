Adoptions Policy

Adoption should protect welfare before, during and after placement.

This page restates the main public adoption-policy themes highlighted in the attached governance review so visitors can understand the expectations around responsible rehoming.

Go to adoptions Back to policy hub
Adopter responsibilityAnimal welfareAftercare expectations
1

Health information

Animals should leave with the health status and known information APES can reasonably provide at the point of adoption.

2

Suitable keepers

Adopters are expected to be suitable, lawful keepers who can meet species-specific welfare needs.

3

Ongoing care

The policy places continuing responsibility for care, housing, food, monitoring and lawful ownership on the adopter.

4

Legal compliance

Adoption must stay aligned with welfare law, licensing rules and any specific requirements tied to the species adopted.

Key policy points

  • Animals are rehomed on the basis that their welfare remains the priority after placement.
  • People disqualified from keeping animals, or otherwise unable to care lawfully and safely, should not adopt.
  • Where relevant to the species or placement terms, neutering and other follow-up obligations may still apply.
  • APES may need to pass personal details to recognised microchip, passport or administrative systems where required.

What this means in practice

  • Read species guidance fully before applying.
  • Be honest about space, heating, time and care experience.
  • Do not treat adoption as a short-term or disposable arrangement.
  • Use APES guidance or support routes if circumstances change after placement.
