Hugo's story

Hugo was hand-raised after a difficult start and later surrendered to APES when his keepers could no longer meet his needs safely. His case highlights how quickly stress, unsuitable spaces and changing home dynamics can affect primate welfare.

Worm's story

Worm came into care from severe neglect and is now in quarantine and slow behavioural recovery. APES is working toward a careful introduction process in the hope that he and Hugo can be paired safely over time.

What your sponsorship pays for

  • Specialist diet and supplements
  • Veterinary checks, diagnostics and preventative treatment
  • Enrichment that encourages climbing, foraging and problem-solving
  • Heat, humidity support, nesting areas and safe furnishings
  • Ongoing improvements to the long-term primate habitat

The next step

APES is planning a purpose-built enclosure with a secure outdoor space linked to a warm indoor area. Sponsorship helps fund framework, mesh, heat, climbing structures, separation areas and rotating enrichment supplies.
