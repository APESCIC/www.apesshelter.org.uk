Re-Homing Policy

Placements should be safe, assessed and welfare-led.

This page brings together the key public re-homing policy themes identified in the governance review.

Animals should be assessed before adoption so APES can make an informed placement decision.

Home suitability

Adopters must be adults and may be subject to home or circumstance checks, including landlord permission where relevant.

4

Responsible boundaries

The policy rules out unsafe or unsuitable routes such as market, prize or experimental placements.

Key policy themes

  • Rehoming decisions should reflect the animal's welfare history, behaviour, health and placement risks.
  • APES should not place animals into arrangements that would compromise welfare through travel, environment or legal issues.
  • Remote, casual or unsuitable placement methods are not the standard expected by the policy.
  • Schools and other environments that do not meet species needs are discouraged or unsuitable for many placements.

Practical takeaways

  • Prepare evidence that your set-up is suitable.
  • Check landlord, licensing and family-consent issues early.
  • Expect questions about experience, space and species knowledge.
  • Use contact routes if you need to understand whether a placement is realistic before applying.

The re-homing principles are about safe, lawful and welfare-led placement. Exact checks vary by animal and route.
