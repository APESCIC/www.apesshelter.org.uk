Hugo sponsorship

Meet Hugo

Sponsorship helps with food, bedding, cleaning, enrichment and enclosure maintenance for one of APES' resident common marmosets.

£30 / month

Hugo's story

From private home to APES care

According to the history shared with APES, Hugo was purchased for a man's son in his 20s. He was reportedly brought into the UK while still in his mother's womb and was born here after his mother rejected him, most likely because of the stress of being taken from the wild and exported. The keeper who had hand-raised his mother then hand-raised Hugo until he was sold to the man and his son.

His son kept him in the enclosure shown above. When his pregnant girlfriend moved into the room, Hugo became increasingly aggressive toward women, including urine-spraying at the girlfriend from across the room while he was in his cage. Unable to find anywhere else for him, they surrendered Hugo to APES.

He now lives safely at APES, where sponsorship helps provide daily care, enrichment and enclosure upkeep.

Worm sponsorship

Meet Worm

Worm's sponsorship helps cover quarantine care, enrichment, food and the slow introduction process with Hugo.

£30 / month

Worm's story

A rescue with limited history

Because of the nature of Worm's rescue, APES cannot share much detail. What can be said is that he was kept in a budgie or cockatiel cage that was no wider or taller than approximately 1 ft by 1 ft by 4 ft.

There was bird droppings and old bird food at the bottom of the cage. A member of the public saw Worm being sold, paid to take him, and then surrendered him to APES so he could have a better life.

Worm came from abusive keepers and had been manhandled and treated so badly that staff who saw video of his former keeping were brought to tears. He is now in quarantine for the next few months, followed by around six months of slow introduction to Hugo in the hope that the two can eventually be paired.
